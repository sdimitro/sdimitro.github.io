Acme: A User Interface for Programmers

Around April 2016 I started playing with Acme, a powerful text editor and development environment developed by Rob Pike. I installed Acme through Russ Cox's plan9port. You can find the latest version of his code here. One thing that I believe that Acme lacks (but not necessarily needs!) is a place that hosts resources that can help newcomers and potentially more advanced users of Acme to become more productive with the editor. This page is an attempt to be that place.

  • Plan9Port at swtch.com
  • Pike's Original Paper

Other Useful Links:

The above may seem too generic but they are actually sufficient to get you started and understand the spirit of Acme. Unfortunately since many of them are posted on blogs and blogs tend to get deleted pretty often, I will try at some point to share my notes/scripts from them online in case they get deleted. One thing at a time :-)
